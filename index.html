Background

About me

I'm a knowledge-driven person whose background is in psychology but whose curiosity lives somewhere between consumer behavior, computer science, and economics. I love the Austrian School, and I believe the intersection of human behavior and quantitative models will reshape how we understand markets and people.

I hold a PhD in Behavioral Economics / Consumer Behavior from Konrad Lorenz University and I'm currently pursuing a Master of Engineering in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Hull. The combination isn't accidental — I want to understand both the power and the limits of models when applied to human systems.

Professionally, I work as a Research Interviewer at IDC, conducting market research and business value interviews. I've also taught at Fanshawe College and Konrad Lorenz University — subjects like behavioral economics, decision science, big data analytics, and business analytics.

On the side, I actively manage a personal investment portfolio, typically generating returns between 5–15% annually. Nothing magical — just discipline, curiosity, and constant learning.

P.S. I'm also a gym rat 💪
